What are geomagnetic waves?

Geomagnetic waves are disturbances in the Earth’s magnetic field. They are usually caused by solar flares and other sudden, drastic changes such as plasma interactions and Earth’s inner currents.

What are the fast lines?

The fast lines zipping across the screen represent all solar winds that happened in the given time frame. The lines pulsate with red and green, indicating the amplitude of effect the Earth's gravitational field has on it.

What is the heatmap?

The heatmap shows the strength of aurora visibility of the region

Note

The simulation only represents solar winds and cases of geomagnetic activity during the month of May in 2024. We chose this month because it had an unusually high amount of geomagnetic activity.
